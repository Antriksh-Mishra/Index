About Food Wastage

Food wastage is a significant issue affecting our environment and economy. Understanding its impact is crucial for making informed choices.

Statistics

  • Every year, about one-third of food produced for human consumption is wasted. This is equivalent to 1.3 billion tons of food!

  • Approximately one-third of all food produced is wasted.

  • Food waste generates about 8-10% of global greenhouse gas emissions.

  • The average family of four wastes about $1,500 a year on food that is never eaten.

  • Nearly 1 billion people around the world are hungry while food waste continues to rise.

Tips to Reduce Waste

  • Plan your meals before shopping.

  • Store food properly to extend its shelf life.

  • Use leftovers creatively.

  • Compost organic waste.

Tips for Reducing Food Waste

Plan Your Meals: Create a meal plan before shopping to avoid buying unnecessary items.

Understand Expiration Dates: Know the difference between "sell by," "use by," and "best before" dates.

Store Food Properly: Learn how to store fruits and vegetables to extend their shelf life.

Compost: Composting food scraps can reduce waste and create nutrient-rich soil.
